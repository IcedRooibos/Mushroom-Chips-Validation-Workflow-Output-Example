## Ad Copy Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

**Voice (Consistent)**:
- Warm and friendly, like a knowledgeable friend
- Confident but humble
- Clear and concise
- Genuine and honest

**Tone (Adjusts by Context)**:
- Educational content: Informative, accessible
- Product descriptions: Sensory, appetizing
- Social media: Conversational, engaging
- Ads: Benefit-focused, action-oriented

### Language Principles

**Do**:
- Use active voice and strong verbs
- Include sensory language (crispy, rich, satisfying)
- Emphasize benefits over features
- Create urgency when appropriate
- Use "you" and "your" to personalize
- Be specific with numbers (5g protein, not "high protein")

**Don't**:
- Use jargon or buzzwords
- Make exaggerated claims
- Guilt-trip or shame
- Compare negatively to competitors
- Use overly trendy language
- Be salesy or pushy

### A/B Testing Variations

**Headlines to Test**:
- Benefit-led vs. Problem-led
- Specific numbers vs. General claims
- Questions vs. Statements
- Short (5-7 words) vs. Long (10-15 words)

**Body Copy to Test**:
- Emotional appeal vs. Rational appeal
- Feature-focused vs. Benefit-focused
- Short (50 words) vs. Long (150 words)
- With social proof vs. Without

**CTAs to Test**:
- "Shop Now" vs. "Try Today" vs. "Discover More"
- "Get Started" vs. "Order Now" vs. "Learn More"
- With urgency vs. Without urgency
